Shipping Container Transport Cost — What to Expect in 2026

Understanding shipping container transport cost in the UK is essential for importers, exporters, and logistics managers who need to budget accurately for their supply chain operations. Unlike many industries where pricing is standardised, container haulage rates vary significantly based on distance, port, timing, and specific requirements. This guide breaks down the typical costs you will encounter when moving containers by road across the UK.

The good news is that UK container transport pricing is generally straightforward once you understand the key variables. The overwhelming majority of the cost comes from two factors — the distance between the port and the delivery address, and the container size (20ft vs 40ft). Everything else — timing, waiting time, access, special requirements — adds relatively modest increments to the base rate.

What Affects Shipping Container Transport Cost?

Distance — The Biggest Factor

Distance from the port to the delivery address accounts for the largest portion of your shipping container transport cost. Longer routes mean more fuel, more driver time, and more vehicle wear. As a rough rule, each additional 50 miles adds approximately £50 to £100 to the base rate. This is why choosing the right port of entry — the one closest to your final destination — can save significant money on the road haulage leg.

Container Size

A 40ft container typically costs 10 to 20 percent more to transport than a 20ft container on the same route. The difference is modest because the main costs (driver wages, fuel, vehicle depreciation) are similar for both sizes. The premium for a 40ft container reflects slightly higher port handling charges and marginally increased fuel consumption due to the greater weight and aerodynamic drag.

Port of Collection

Different ports have different access charges, VBS (Vehicle Booking System) costs, and turnaround times that affect the total shipping container transport cost. Felixstowe, as the UK's busiest port, has well-established procedures but can sometimes involve longer waiting times during peak periods. Smaller ports may have lower terminal charges but potentially fewer haulage options.

Timing and Urgency

Standard bookings made 24 to 48 hours in advance attract the best rates. Same-day or next-day collections typically carry a 15 to 30 percent premium. Collections and deliveries outside standard hours (evenings, weekends, bank holidays) may incur overtime surcharges of £50 to £150 depending on the provider.

Waiting Time

Most container haulage quotes include 1 to 2 hours of free waiting time at both the port and the delivery address. If the collection or delivery takes longer — due to port congestion, customs delays, or slow unloading at the warehouse — additional charges apply, typically £30 to £50 per hour.

Delivery Access

If the delivery site has restricted access — narrow roads, tight turning circles, low bridges, or weight-limited routes — the haulier may need to use alternative vehicles or routes, which increases the shipping container transport cost. Sites that cannot accommodate a standard articulated lorry may require transhipment to a smaller vehicle, adding significant cost.

How to Get the Best Container Transport Rate

Several practical steps can help you secure competitive shipping container transport costs. Book as early as possible — last-minute bookings attract premium rates. Be flexible with timing — allowing the haulier to schedule your collection around their other movements can result in lower rates. Ensure your delivery site is ready to receive the container — having a forklift, loading dock, or clear space for the trailer reduces waiting time charges. Provide accurate and complete information when requesting quotes — container size, weight, port, PIN number, and delivery postcode all help the haulier quote accurately first time.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does it cost to transport a shipping container in the UK?
Typically £250 to £800 per container, depending on port, distance, and container size. A 40ft container from Felixstowe to the Midlands costs approximately £400–£550.
What factors affect shipping container transport cost?
Distance, container size (20ft vs 40ft), port of collection, timing/urgency, waiting time, and delivery access restrictions are the main factors.
Is a 20ft container cheaper to transport than a 40ft?
Yes, typically 10–20% cheaper on the same route. The difference is modest because driver time and fuel are similar for both sizes.
Are there hidden charges in container transport costs?
Reputable companies provide transparent pricing. Watch for port waiting time charges, detention fees for late empty returns, and access surcharges for restricted delivery sites.
How can I get the cheapest shipping container transport rate?
Book in advance, be flexible on timing, ensure clear site access, provide accurate container details, and consider volume contracts for regular imports.
