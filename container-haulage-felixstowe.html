Container Haulage From the Port of Felixstowe

The Port of Felixstowe is the largest container port in the United Kingdom, handling approximately 4 million TEUs (twenty-foot equivalent units) annually and accounting for roughly 36% of all containerised trade entering and leaving the country. Situated on the Suffolk coast, Felixstowe is also one of the busiest container ports in Europe, receiving vessels from major international shipping lines connecting the UK to markets across Asia, the Americas, the Middle East, and beyond.

Understanding the Felixstowe Vehicle Booking System (VBS)

The Vehicle Booking System, or VBS, is a mandatory pre-booking platform used at the Port of Felixstowe to manage the flow of haulage vehicles entering and exiting the terminal. Every carrier collecting or delivering a container at Felixstowe must book a VBS slot in advance. The system assigns specific time windows for vehicle arrivals, which helps the port manage congestion, optimise terminal operations, and maintain safety standards across the facility.

For carriers unfamiliar with the VBS process, it can be a source of confusion and delays. Arriving without a valid booking means being turned away from the gate. Arriving outside your allocated time window can result in slot cancellation and the need to rebook, adding hours or even days to the collection process. Understanding how to work within the VBS system — including booking amendment procedures, slot availability patterns, and the best times to schedule collections — is a critical skill for any haulage company operating regular container haulage Felixstowe services.

Rincon Services manages all VBS bookings as part of our standard container haulage service. Our operations team monitors slot availability, books optimal collection times based on container availability notifications, and adjusts bookings proactively if vessel schedules change or delays occur. This end-to-end VBS management ensures our customers never need to worry about the complexities of port booking systems — we handle everything from container release to final delivery.

Felixstowe Terminals & Port Operations

The Port of Felixstowe operates two main container terminals, each with its own characteristics and operational procedures that carriers must understand.

Trinity Terminal is the larger of the two facilities, handling the majority of deep-sea container traffic at Felixstowe. It accommodates the world's largest container vessels and processes high volumes of import and export containers daily. Trinity Terminal has undergone significant expansion and modernisation in recent years, with upgraded quay cranes and expanded yard capacity to handle growing trade volumes.

Landguard Terminal serves as the secondary container terminal, handling both deep-sea and short-sea container traffic. Landguard operates slightly differently from Trinity, with its own gate procedures and yard layout. Carriers operating at Landguard need to understand these differences to ensure smooth collections and avoid unnecessary delays.

In addition to the main terminals, Felixstowe has several supporting facilities including the Felixstowe South reconfiguration area, container freight stations for destuffing and stuffing operations, and connections to the A14 trunk road providing direct access to the national motorway network. Rincon Services operates across all of these facilities, ensuring complete coverage of container haulage Felixstowe operations.

Avoiding Demurrage & Detention Charges at Felixstowe

One of the most significant hidden costs in container shipping is demurrage and detention charges. Demurrage is charged by the port when a container remains in the terminal beyond the allowed free storage period. Detention is charged by the shipping line when the container itself is not returned within the specified timeframe. Both charges accumulate daily and can quickly add substantial costs to an import or export shipment.

The most effective way to minimise these charges is to work with a container haulage Felixstowe provider that can collect containers quickly once they become available and return empties promptly after destuffing. Rincon Services excels in this area because our 24/7 operations, large fleet capacity, and proactive VBS management mean we can respond rapidly to container availability notifications and get your goods moving without delay.

Our dispatch team monitors container release dates and shipping line free time allowances, scheduling collections to maximise the free period and avoid any unnecessary charges. We also manage empty container returns efficiently, ensuring containers are back at the port or designated depot well within the shipping line's allowed timeframe.
