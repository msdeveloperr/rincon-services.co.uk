What Are Road Freight Services?

Road freight services encompass the commercial transportation of goods by truck, lorry, or van across the road network. In the United Kingdom, road freight is the dominant mode of goods transport, accounting for approximately 76 percent of all domestic freight moved by tonnage. Every product you see on a shop shelf, every component in a factory, and every parcel delivered to a home has almost certainly travelled by road at some point in its journey.

The scope of road freight services is broad. It includes everything from a single pallet moving between two warehouses to a fleet of articulated lorries running daily scheduled routes between distribution centres. Container haulage from ports, temperature-controlled food deliveries, hazardous materials transport, and e-commerce fulfilment logistics all fall under the umbrella of road freight services in the UK.

For businesses, road freight offers a combination of flexibility, speed, and accessibility that no other transport mode can match. Unlike rail freight, which requires access to railway terminals, or sea freight, which only works between ports, road freight can reach virtually any address in the country. This door-to-door capability makes road freight services the natural choice for the vast majority of UK businesses, from small manufacturers to multinational retailers.

Types of Road Freight Transport in the UK

Container Haulage

Container haulage is a specialised form of road freight that involves transporting shipping containers from ports to inland destinations and back. This service requires HGV tractor units equipped with skeletal trailers designed to carry standard 20ft and 40ft containers. Container haulage is the critical link between international sea freight and domestic distribution, and it demands hauliers with port knowledge, Vehicle Booking System (VBS) experience, and efficient logistics planning to avoid port charges like demurrage.

General Haulage and Curtainside Freight

General haulage uses curtainside trailers — vehicles with flexible sides that can be opened from either side for easy loading — to move palletised goods, loose cargo, and commercial freight between warehouses, factories, and distribution centres. Curtainside vehicles are the most versatile road freight option because they allow loading from the side, rear, or even overhead with a crane. Most UK general haulage operates on full truckload or part-load basis.

Flatbed and Open Transport

Flatbed trailers carry oversized, heavy, or awkward items that cannot fit inside an enclosed trailer. Construction materials, steel beams, timber, and large machinery commonly travel on flatbed vehicles. Some loads require additional securing with chains, straps, or specialised fixtures, and drivers must hold specific training to handle these loads safely.

Temperature-Controlled Freight

Refrigerated vehicles, commonly called reefers, maintain specific temperature ranges during transit. This road freight service is essential for perishable food products, pharmaceuticals, and chemical goods that degrade outside controlled temperatures. The UK's food industry relies heavily on temperature-controlled road freight to move goods from processing plants to supermarket distribution centres.

Amazon Relay and E-Commerce Logistics

The explosion of online shopping has created a new category of road freight services. Amazon Relay is Amazon's own freight programme that contracts with approved carriers to move goods between fulfilment centres, sortation facilities, and last-mile delivery stations. This high-volume, time-sensitive road freight requires carriers with excellent on-time performance records and the ability to operate seven days a week.

FTL vs LTL — Choosing the Right Road Freight Service

Full Truck Load (FTL)

A full truck load means your goods occupy the entire vehicle from collection to delivery. You are effectively hiring exclusive use of the truck for your shipment. FTL road freight services are the fastest option because the vehicle drives directly from origin to destination without stopping to collect or deliver other consignments. FTL is the most cost-effective choice when you have enough cargo to fill or nearly fill a trailer — typically 20 or more pallets, or goods weighing 15 tonnes or above.

The advantages of FTL are clear — faster transit times, reduced handling (your goods are loaded once and unloaded once), lower risk of damage, and guaranteed capacity. For time-sensitive road freight or high-value goods, FTL provides the security and predictability that supply chain managers need.

Less Than Truck Load (LTL) and Groupage

When your shipment does not fill an entire vehicle, less than truck load services allow you to share trailer space with other consignments heading in the same direction. LTL road freight — sometimes called groupage or part-load — is cheaper per shipment than FTL, making it ideal for smaller quantities of freight. However, transit times are longer because the vehicle makes multiple stops for collections and deliveries, and there is a slightly higher risk of handling damage due to the additional loading and unloading.

The break-even point between FTL and LTL typically sits around 10 to 15 pallets. Below that, LTL is usually more economical. Above that threshold, the per-pallet rate of FTL often works out cheaper, and you gain the benefits of direct delivery and reduced handling.

Why Road Freight Dominates UK Logistics

The United Kingdom moves approximately 1.5 billion tonnes of goods by road each year, and there are compelling reasons why road freight services remain the backbone of the national supply chain despite growing interest in rail and sustainable alternatives.

Flexibility and Reach

The UK has over 262,000 miles of paved roads, meaning road freight can reach any business, warehouse, factory, or residential address in the country. No other transport mode offers this level of accessibility. A lorry can collect from a farm in rural Wales and deliver to a distribution centre in central England within hours, without requiring any special infrastructure at either end.

Speed for Domestic Distances

For the distances involved in UK domestic transport — the longest mainland journey is approximately 600 miles from Land's End to John o'Groats — road freight is almost always the fastest option. Most deliveries between major commercial hubs take under 8 hours by road, and same-day services are available for urgent consignments. Rail freight, while efficient for high-volume point-to-point movements, cannot match this speed for door-to-door delivery.

Scalability

Road freight services scale easily. Need to move one pallet? A van can handle it. Need to move 26 pallets? An articulated lorry is ready. Need to move 200 containers from port? A fleet can be mobilised. This scalability makes road freight adaptable to seasonal demand fluctuations, unexpected surges, and growing businesses that need transport solutions that expand alongside them.

Road Freight Costs and Pricing Factors

The cost of road freight services in the UK depends on several interrelated factors. Understanding these helps you budget accurately and negotiate effectively with transport providers.

Distance

Distance is the primary cost driver. A delivery from Felixstowe to a warehouse in the Midlands (approximately 150 miles) will cost significantly less than a delivery from Southampton to Scotland (approximately 450 miles). Most haulage companies price based on mileage with a minimum charge for short trips.

Vehicle Type and Size

The vehicle required affects the rate. An articulated lorry with a 13.6-metre trailer costs more to operate than a rigid truck. Specialised vehicles — refrigerated, ADR-compliant for hazardous goods, or HIAB-equipped for self-loading — carry premium rates due to higher purchase and maintenance costs.

Load Characteristics

Heavy goods cost more per mile than light goods because fuel consumption increases with weight. Awkward or oversized items that require special handling, additional securing, or escort vehicles also attract higher charges. Hazardous goods transport requires ADR-trained drivers and specialised equipment, adding to the cost.

Urgency and Timing

Same-day and next-day road freight services command premium rates compared to standard 48-hour or economy services. Weekend and bank holiday collections and deliveries also attract surcharges. Planning ahead and booking with reasonable lead times is the simplest way to secure competitive road freight rates.

How to Choose a Road Freight Provider

Selecting the right road freight services partner is a decision that directly impacts your supply chain reliability, customer satisfaction, and transport costs. Here are the key criteria to evaluate.

Fleet Size and Capability

A larger fleet means greater availability and the ability to scale during busy periods. Check that the provider has appropriate vehicles for your cargo type — skeletal trailers for containers, curtainsides for general freight, or temperature-controlled units for perishables. A company with a diverse fleet can serve multiple needs without subcontracting.

Geographic Coverage

Ensure the provider covers your collection and delivery locations efficiently. A haulier based in the centre of the country, like Rincon Services in Bedfordshire, can reach most UK destinations within a reasonable driving time. Providers based at the geographic extremes may need to dead-leg (run empty) over long distances, which gets reflected in higher rates.

Reliability and Communication

On-time delivery performance is perhaps the most important metric for any road freight services provider. Ask potential partners about their on-time rates, how they handle delays, and what communication systems they have in place. GPS tracking, proactive delay notifications, and a responsive operations team are hallmarks of a professional road freight company.
